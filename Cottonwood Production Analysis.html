Executive Summary

This analysis examines whether the production shortfall at Peak Family Dental Care Cottonwood is attributable to marketing (patient flow), clinical (diagnosis/acceptance), or operational (team execution/scheduling) factors. The data from Q1 2025 vs Q1 2026 reveals a complex picture where the core assumption—that marketing is the problem—requires substantial reframing.

New patient volume increased dramatically: +48% (112 to 166 patients). This is not a marketing failure—it is a marketing success. The real production challenges center on how those patients are being converted, scheduled, and retained in the system.

Key findings: While case dollars presented increased 69.5%, case dollars accepted rose only 25.7%. The case acceptance rate on total production fell 15.7 percentage points. Pre-appointment rates collapsed from 48% to 0%. These metrics suggest that the constraint is not patient volume—it is conversion, scheduling efficiency, and operational execution.

What we see instead is inconsistency between volume presented and volume accepted—a classic sign of communication breakdown or financial barriers.

What the Data Actually Shows

  • Marketing: On Track. New patient volume is up 48%, contradicting the assertion that marketing is broken. The pipeline is healthy and full. This is not the problem.

  • Doctor/Diagnosis: Mixed Signals. The doctor is diagnosing more restorative work, but presenting 69.5% more in case dollars while acceptance rates are falling. The issue is not that treatment opportunities are being missed—it is that the presentation is not converting patients to treatment.

  • Team/Process: At Risk (PRIMARY CONCERN). Pre-appointment rates have collapsed to zero. Hygiene re-appointment is only 29.5%. Appointment confirmation is below 50%. Recaptured patients are down. The operations are not capturing and scheduling the demand that marketing and the doctor are generating.

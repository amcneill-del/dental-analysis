Executive Summary

This analysis examines whether production challenges at Peak Family Dental Care Sedona stem from marketing (patient flow), clinical (diagnosis and case acceptance), or operational (team execution and scheduling) factors.

The data reveals a mixed picture. While new patient volume increased modestly (80 to 97 patients, +21%), the location is dealing with significant operational challenges that are constraining growth and production. Specifically, the practice has eliminated its unscheduled treatment backlog, which had been a $977K opportunity — but this dramatic swing suggests data inconsistency rather than genuine improvement. At the same time, schedule capacity and team confirmation rates have declined, and patient value metrics show erosion despite higher case acceptance rates.

The doctor's complaint about needing more new patients is partially valid, but the data shows that operational and diagnostic efficiency are equally or more significant limiting factors.

Appointments broken increased 58% (71 to 112), and cancellations surged 88% (52 to 98). These are red flags. Step 1 — Validate:

Let's look at whether patient flow is the issue.

Data talking points:

  • New patient volume grew 21% (80 to 97 patients)

  • Cases presented increased 40% ($548K to $769K)

  • New patient case acceptance jumped to 88%

Step 2 — Reframe:

Production growth is being constrained by team execution, not patient volume.

Specific operational gaps:

  • Appointment confirmation rate dropped 14 points (58% to 43%), directly correlating with a 58% surge in broken appointments

  • Cancellations doubled (52 to 98), indicating patients are backing out after scheduling

  • Pre-appointing at hygiene dropped to 0%, disrupting the reappointment funnel

  • Patient value per restorative exam declined 16%, despite higher case acceptance

Step 3 — Action:

Focus improvement efforts on appointment confirmation and hygiene pre-appointing. Marketing is not the limiting factor.

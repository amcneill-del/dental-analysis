Production Analysis Report
Flagstaff
Q1 2025 vs Q1 2026

Executive Summary

This analysis examines whether the production differences at Flagstaff between Q1 2025 and Q1 2026 are attributable to marketing (patient flow), clinical (diagnosis/acceptance), or operational (team execution/scheduling) factors.

Key Finding:

New patient volume is UP 42% (88 to 125 patients), which demonstrates strong marketing performance. However, dollar production metrics show mixed signals: case dollars presented and accepted are down 9%, same-day treatment is down 24%, and appointments per working day dropped 14%. The production scheduled gross is up 70%, but this tells a complex story.

The data suggests the challenge is NOT patient quality or patient flow — it's execution across diagnosis, scheduling efficiency, and same-day treatment capture. Marketing delivered more patients; the question is whether the operational infrastructure is fully converting that volume.

Analysis: Overall case acceptance is STRONG and improving. The overall acceptance rate increased from 80.8% to 84.1% — this is healthy. However, there is a critical discrepancy: new patient case acceptance declined 2.3 points (85.4% to 83.1%), while overall acceptance rose 3.3 points.

This pattern suggests that while new patients are accepting at slightly lower rates, existing/established patients are accepting at higher rates overall. The dollars presented and accepted are both down ~9%, which points to either (a) smaller treatment plans being diagnosed to the newer cohort, or (b) a shift in the case mix being presented.

Key concern: If more patients are coming through the door but presenting dollars are down, it implies the doctor may be under-diagnosing or under-presenting to the expanded patient pool, rather than patients declining cases.

Analysis: The schedule picture is complex. Appointments scheduled nearly doubled (up 88%), which suggests aggressive booking of treatment. However, visits per working day fell 14%. This paradox is explained by the data pattern: the practice is scheduling MORE appointments (production capacity up 70%), but seeing fewer visits per day in Q1 2026.

Possible interpretation: The practice may have shifted toward longer treatment appointments (more complex cases, implant consultations) or opened additional room time, resulting in fewer daily visit encounters but more total production value. The 70% jump in production scheduled gross supports this — the pipeline has expanded significantly.
